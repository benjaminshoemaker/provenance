High-Impact UI Modifications

Four targeted changes based on convergent findings from three independent competitive analyses. Each addresses a specific trust, comprehension, or usability risk.

Unified Origin Color System — Used Across All Surfaces

Human-written gray-700
AI-generated violet-500
Pasted orange-400
Same colors in badges, stacked bars, timeline events, and badge preview origin marks.

Neutral Badge Colors with Origin Breakdown

Remove the green→red judgment scale. Use the unified origin colors (human/AI/paste) to show the three-way composition instead of a single "AI %" that implies good/bad.

Before — Green-to-red color scale

Reader instinct: "Green = good writer, red = lazy." This frames AI usage as inherently negative.

After — Neutral brand color, consistent regardless of %

Same badge color at every percentage — "verified process," not "acceptable score"

Every badge says the same thing: "process recorded." The % is data, not a verdict.

Inline AI: "Keep Original" as a First-Class Choice

Shift from "accept/reject this AI change" to "choose among options including your own text." The writer's original is always a valid choice, not just the absence of action.

Before — Accept/Reject binary

In a world where anyone can generate a passable blog post in seconds, I find myself returning to the keyboard with renewed purpose.

AI Suggestion Simplify

Writing forces you to confront the gaps in your understanding. When you sit down to explain something, you quickly discover what you don't actually know. Writing reveals what you don't actually understand. The moment you try to explain something clearly, you discover where your knowledge falls short.

Accept/Reject frames AI as the default. Original text isn't visible as a choice. Diff shows only the AI option.

After — Choose among equals (including original)

In a world where anyone can generate a passable blog post in seconds, I find myself returning to the keyboard with renewed purpose.

Co-Locate Methodology Next to the Number

When the definition is in a separate collapsed section below the fold, most viewers never see it. The meaning of "12% AI" must be immediately adjacent via an inline tooltip.

Before — Methodology buried below the fold
12%
AI-Generated
↕ content between stat and explanation
How the AI percentage is calculated
The AI-generated percentage represents the proportion of final document characters that originated from accepted AI suggestions...

Most viewers see the number, form an opinion, and never scroll to the methodology.

After — Definition inline, immediately accessible
12% AI-assisted How is this calculated? About this percentage Represents the proportion of final text that originated from accepted AI suggestions and remained substantially unchanged (<20% modification). Text typed by the author, or AI text modified by more than 20%, is classified as human-written. Does not account for AI used outside this tool, pre-writing research, or ideas from AI conversation typed manually.

Four changes, one principle: Provenance records the process without judging it.

On Write/Review mode (originally Change 4): Downgraded. Since Provenance isn't the publishing platform, persistent editor origin marks serve as self-awareness tooling with no functional output. The stronger version: origin marks surface during the pre-badge review flow (which the existing badge preview mockup already handles) rather than as a persistent editor toggle.
